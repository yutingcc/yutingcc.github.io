The Water Sprinkling Festival

This project showcases The Water Sprinkling Festival's activities, aiming to capture the vibrant festival ambiance and provide a glimpse into China's Dai culture.

The composition features elements symbolizing the festival's essence. Kongming lanterns dot the sky, heralding the festival's arrival. A quarter-circle on the right displays Yunnan's map, pinpointing the festival's location. Three ground elements—elephant (Yunnan's emblematic animal), stilted house (local architecture), and mountain (regional landscape)—embody the area's distinctiveness. A peacock and a Dai girl at the forefront depict water splashing. The Dai girl's portrayal was inspired by Shaoguang Ding's influential ethnic painting, applying principles of asymmetry, scale, and proportion for dynamic balance.

The animated version aims to recreate the festival's vibrancy. It commences with lanterns descending, akin to a top-down camera movement. Animated elements exhibit a lively bounce upon entry. Water splashes were informed by video research, leveraging particle systems. Converging into a river, the water carries Yunnan, symbolizing its water surroundings. Realistic water fluctuations are achieved with AE's fractal noise. The animation then zooms, focusing on the water's ripples, bathed in the region's reflective hues.
